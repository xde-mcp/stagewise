## How We Got Here

stagewise started with a clear idea: let product builders and developers point at any element on their app and tell an AI agent to change it. No digging through code. No context switching. Just point, describe, ship.

That idea resonated. Developers and product people used stagewise to move faster, ship smaller changes confidently, and stay out of the weeds.

But as we watched how people actually worked, a pattern emerged. The best sessions happened when you could see your app *and* pull inspiration from the web *and* work alongside the agent — all at once. Stitching that together across a browser, a terminal, and an IDE was the real bottleneck. Not the idea. The environment.

So we rebuilt from the ground up.

---

## What Changed

**Before:** stagewise lived alongside your tools — a plugin that connected your app to an agent, sitting between your browser and your IDE.

**Now:** stagewise *is* the environment. A browser-native co-working platform where your app under development, the open web, and your agent share the same workspace.

---

## What You Get

### Everything in one place

Your app, the web, your codebase, and your agent — all in a single window. Draw inspiration from any site, then implement it directly. No tab juggling, no proxy setup, no CORS surprises.

### An agent that sees what you see

Your agent isn't running blind in a terminal. It's looking at exactly what you're looking at — the page you're building, the reference you found, the component you want to change. It iterates *with* you, not ahead of you.

### End-to-end, not just implementation

Ideation, design, code, and marketing copy — stagewise supports the full arc of building a product. Skills like copywriting, design patterns, and code conventions are first-class citizens in the platform.

### Your model, your keys, your rules

Choose your own model. Bring your own API key. Connect custom inference providers. Full control over the AI layer, without compromising the experience.

## How It Works

1. **Open stagewise** - your browser, your agent, your workspace, ready to go
2. **Point at anything** — your app, a reference site, a design detail — and describe what you want
3. **Iterate together** — the agent makes changes, you review in real time, and you ship when it's right
